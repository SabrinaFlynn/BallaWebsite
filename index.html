Balla, Co Mayo

This page is a dedicated space for the documentation of the history culture and heritage of a small village in County Mayo.

The Balla Community

Balla is a modern village which was originally developed around the site of a seventh century monastery founded by St Crónán. The holy well that inspired St Crónán to found his monastic settlement and church has recently been restored along with a 17th Century building that was used as a rest house for passing pilgrims.

Balla has a thriving community dedicated to preserving, recording and promoting the history of the town.

Their efforts have been noticed and they have recently won first place at the 'Pride of Place' awards

The Croagh Patrick Heritage Trail is a 61km path through the Mayo countyside. It starts in Balla, and run right through to Murrisk at the foot of Croagh Patrick.

The Round Tower in the Centre of Balla is all that remains of the original settlement of St Crónán.

Though it is said that it was three times this height when built.
